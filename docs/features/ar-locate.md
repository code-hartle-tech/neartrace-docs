# AR Locate & Sonar

> **S01E08 — "Hot or cold. But make it science."**

<div class="tldr">
<strong>TL;DR</strong>
AR Locate uses your camera + RSSI to visually guide you toward a device. Sonar mode plays a proximity-modulated ping — stronger signal, faster pings. Both modes help you physically locate a device you know is nearby.
</div>

## AR Locate

Open a device's detail page → tap **AR Locate**.

Your camera view appears with an RSSI ring overlay. Walk around — the ring's size reflects signal strength. Move toward the device and the ring grows. Move away and it shrinks.

Not GPS navigation. Not precise triangulation. Think of it as a hot/cold game powered by actual radio physics.

**Best for:** Finding a device you know is within 10 metres — a drone in tall grass, earbuds under a couch cushion, a tracker someone hid in your bag.

## Sonar Mode

Open a device's detail page → tap **Sonar**.

Your screen goes dark. A submarine-style sonar ping plays — synthesised at 720 Hz with a second harmonic and exponential decay. The ping rate increases as signal strength increases.

Slow pings = far. Fast pings = close. Silence = you lost it.

**Best for:** Searching in the dark, in a bag, or eyes-up situations where you can't stare at the screen.

::: tip
Sonar plays under your media volume. Turn it up before you start.
:::
